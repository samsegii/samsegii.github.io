The Problem

MASSIVE ENERGY
LOST EVERY DAY

Water distribution systems require high pressure to move water across elevation changes. When that water reaches customers, pressure reducing valves (PRVs) are legally required — but they dissipate all that energy as heat, wasted forever.

10B+

Gallons Daily

California alone moves over 10 billion gallons of water through high-pressure pipeline systems every single day — creating enormous untapped energy potential at every pressure zone boundary.

Millions

Of Valves, Lost Energy

PRVs are installed throughout every water distribution system globally. Each one is a point of energy destruction. Every drop of pressure differential is converted to waste heat — 24 hours a day, 365 days a year.

How It Works

PRESSURE IN.
POWER OUT.

A four-step process that converts wasted pressure drop into grid-ready electricity — while maintaining perfect downstream pressure for customers.

01

High-Pressure Inlet

High-pressure water enters the Hydropulse unit at the PRV station — replacing or bypassing the existing valve.

02

Turbine Spins

Water pressure spins the pump-as-turbine impeller. The PAT converts hydraulic pressure energy into rotational mechanical energy.

03

Electricity Generated

The turbine drives an induction generator. The "Pulse Brain" controller ensures maximum efficiency while maintaining target downstream pressure.

04

Grid-Ready Power

Clean electricity is fed back to the local grid or facility. Water exits at correct service pressure — no disruption to any downstream process.
